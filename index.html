Graduate Research Assistant in The Human and Computing Cognition Lab at Penn State University

I am a PhD Candidate in Computer Science at Penn State University. Previously, I worked as a full-stack Software Engineer at Cotiviti for four years. I am interested in computational cognitive modeling, specifically how it interacts with sociocultural constructs. I am also fascinated by how artificial intelligence can benefit from a cognitive perspective to achieve metacognition. Because I care deeply about human thinking, behavior, perceptions, and bias, my goal is to develop socioculturally competent AI systems.

How Support Persona Customization Shapes Support Quality

Summary: A comparative study on whether or not customizating support persona type in emotional support seeking improved the perceived support qualtity from chatbot. Result: A between-subjects experiment (N = 60) showed that customization indirectly improved support quality through perceived control -- customization increased users’ perceived control, which in turn was positively associated with support quality.
